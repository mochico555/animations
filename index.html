Sleep

by Eric Whitacre

Sleep, composed by Eric Whitacre, puts the audience into a calming trance. Almost as if it were a lullaby.
The song intricately explains the process of falling asleep with great imagery and gradual criciendos and decreciendos throughout the song to carry the listener through the whole song.


I chose this song as my inspiration because it was one of the songs in our competition set my senior year of high school.
Each form of animation is based on either the theme or a certain verse of the song.

The Sprite is of a character named Totoro as he sleeps on the ground as a curious little girl watches him nap.

The Animation is based on the first stanza, "The evening hangs beneath the moon". This first line made me think of a baby mobile that would help an infant fall asleep. If you hover over the Moon, Stars, and Planets, they will swing back and forth.

Finally, the Transition is inspired by the line saying, "Where clouds of dreams give second sight". To portray this, the page first loads as a white screen with a pair of eyes with stars in the pupils. But hover over the eyes and we see the full image as the eyes slowly close begins to dream.
